# Synthesis: <Text Title>

> Two parts. **Part I** is an authoritative account of the text on its own
> terms — written as if the discussion never happened. **Part II** records what,
> if anything, the discussion *added* to our understanding of the text. The job
> here is to capture how the readers contributed — not to recount the meeting.

## Part I — The text

> A fresh, definitive summary of what the text actually argues. No discussion,
> no annotations, no opinions from the room — just the text.

## Part II — What the discussion contributed

> For each kind of contribution below, record what the readers actually added to
> understanding the text — citing who, drawing on both the annotations and the
> transcript. **Be honest:** if a category produced nothing, say so plainly
> ("Nothing substantive here"). The value of this section is partly in admitting
> where the discussion did *not* move the needle.

### New angles on the thesis

<Readings, distinctions, or reframings that changed how the thesis is

### Connections drawn

### Objections and challenges

### Stronger defenses proposed

<Ways the readers shored up or extended the argument beyond what the text itself

### Questions left open

<Genuine questions the discussion surfaced but did not resolve — or "None.">

### Overall contribution

<A candid one-paragraph verdict: how much did this discussion advance
understanding of the text? Name the load-bearing contribution(s), and don't
inflate a thin discussion into a rich one.>
